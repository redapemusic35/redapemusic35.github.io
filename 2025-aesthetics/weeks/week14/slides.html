Three Critiques:

  • Pure Reason: metaphysics and epistemology
  • Practical Reason: ethics and morality
  • Judgment: aesthetics and teleology

It seems weird that he would put aesthetics and teleology together.

  • Aesthetics: judgments of beauty and sublimity
  • Teleology: judgments of purpose and function in nature

  1. Faculty or the Power of Judgment

    • universals
    • particulars

In the critique of pure reason, Kant discusses how we use our faculty of understanding to form concepts of universals from particulars.

  • faculty of understanding is that cognitive faculty that allows us to look at a particular object, and develop a universal category under which all objects of that kind can fall.

  • consider classes of objects: all dogs with spots, all tables, all beautiful things

  • consider particular objects: this dog with spots, this table, this beautiful thing

The more distinctive role assigned to judgment in the Critique of Judgment is the reflecting role, that of ‚Äúfinding‚Äù the universal for the given particular (Introduction IV, 5:179). (Ginsborg 2022)

  • So the kind of judgment employed with developing universals from particulars is called reflective judgment.

  • In the empirical sciences, this judgment is responsible for classifying natural things into genera and species.

  • This also applies to our use of imagination

In order to decide whether or not something is beautiful, we do not relate the representation by means of understanding to the object for cognition, but rather relate it by means of the imagination (perhaps combined with the understanding) to the subject and its feeling of pleasure or displeasure. (Aesthetics: A Comprehensive Anthology 2020, 155)

But reflective judgment is also responsible for:

  • Aesthetic: the beautiful and the sublime
  • Teleological: purpose and function in nature
